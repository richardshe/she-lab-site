# Introduction [306 words]

Genetic perturbation has been central to assigning gene function and dissecting cellular circuits. In mammalian cells, pooled genetic screens have made it possible to assay thousands of perturbations in parallel, using enrichment or depletion of guide RNAs to identify genes that affect growth, survival, or selectable marker phenotypes. However, many cellular phenotypes are not readily reduced to a single measurement. Regulatory programs, differentiation trajectories, and responses to environmental cues are encoded in coordinated changes across many genes and cell states, requiring high-content molecular readouts that have typically been restricted to arrayed experiments.

Single-cell RNA sequencing offers a natural readout for such screens, because it measures both gene expression and cell-to-cell heterogeneity at scale. In principle, a single pooled experiment could connect each genetic perturbation to a whole-transcriptome phenotype, while also capturing variation in cell quality, cell cycle, differentiation state, and response kinetics. The key challenge is to recover, for each profiled cell, the identity of the perturbation it carries and to separate its effect from the many technical and biological covariates intrinsic to single-cell measurements.

Here, we develop Perturb-seq, a platform that combines CRISPR-based pooled perturbations with single-cell RNA sequencing. We encode each single-guide RNA with an expressed guide barcode, allowing the perturbation identity and transcriptional state of each cell to be read out together. By varying infection multiplicity, the same framework can assay single perturbations or combinations. We further introduce a computational model to estimate the effects of perturbations on genes, signatures, and cell states, while accounting for cell quality, perturbation efficacy, and endogenous heterogeneity.

We apply Perturb-seq to approximately 200,000 cells across primary bone marrow-derived dendritic cells responding to lipopolysaccharide and K562 cells. These experiments identify transcription factor modules, regulated gene programs, altered cellular states, and genetic interactions. Perturb-seq thus links genotype to rich molecular phenotype in pooled screens, providing a scalable route to causal dissection of mammalian regulatory circuits.
