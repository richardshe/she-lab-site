The presence of microbial or host DNA in the cytoplasm of mammalian cells serves as a potent danger signal, alerting the innate immune system to infection or cellular damage. This ectopic DNA triggers a robust immune response characterized by the production of type I interferons. While the adaptor protein STING is known to be essential for transmitting this DNA-dependent signal to the transcription factor IRF3, the precise identity of the upstream cytosolic DNA sensor and the mechanism by which it relays the signal to STING have remained elusive. Here, we identify cyclic GMP-AMP synthase (cGAS) as the critical cytosolic DNA sensor that detects DNA and activates STING by synthesizing a novel second messenger, cyclic GMP-AMP (cGAMP).
