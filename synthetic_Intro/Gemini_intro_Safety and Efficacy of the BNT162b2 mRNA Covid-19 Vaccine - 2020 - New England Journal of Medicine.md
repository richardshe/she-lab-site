The emergence of severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) in late 2019 has precipitated a devastating global health crisis. The resulting disease, coronavirus disease 2019 (Covid-19), rapidly evolved into a pandemic, causing unprecedented morbidity, mortality, and socioeconomic disruption worldwide. As the virus continues to spread, the rapid development and deployment of safe and effective vaccines are urgently required to control the pandemic and mitigate its catastrophic impacts.

SARS-CoV-2 is an enveloped, single-stranded RNA virus belonging to the Coronaviridae family. Its entry into host cells is mediated by the viral spike (S) glycoprotein, which binds to the angiotensin-converting enzyme 2 (ACE2) receptor on human cells. The S protein is a primary target for neutralizing antibodies, making it a critical antigen for vaccine development. Multiple vaccine platforms, including viral vectors, inactivated viruses, and recombinant proteins, have been rapidly mobilized. Among these, lipid nanoparticle-formulated messenger RNA (mRNA) vaccines have emerged as a highly versatile and rapid technology, capable of inducing robust humoral and cellular immune responses without the need for live virus handling during manufacturing.

We previously reported the phase 1 results of two mRNA vaccine candidates, BNT162b1 and BNT162b2. Both candidates elicited strong, dose-dependent SARS-CoV-2-neutralizing antibody responses and T-cell responses in healthy adults. However, BNT162b2, which encodes the full-length prefusion stabilized SARS-CoV-2 spike protein, demonstrated a more favorable safety and reactogenicity profile, particularly in older adults. Consequently, BNT162b2 was selected for advancement into pivotal, large-scale efficacy testing.

Here, we report the safety and efficacy results from the ongoing multinational, placebo-controlled, observer-blinded, phase 2/3 trial of the BNT162b2 vaccine. The primary objectives of this study were to evaluate the efficacy of a two-dose regimen of BNT162b2 in preventing symptomatic Covid-19 in individuals 16 years of age or older, and to characterize its safety profile across diverse demographic subgroups. The data presented herein represent a critical milestone in the global effort to establish a highly effective prophylactic intervention against SARS-CoV-2 infection.
