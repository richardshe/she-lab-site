# Introduction
Mechanical stimuli play a critical role in driving a wide array of fundamental physiological processes, including the sensations of touch and pain, hearing, and the regulation of blood pressure. In various cell types, researchers have recorded the activity of mechanically-activated (MA) cation channels that transduce these physical forces into electrical signals. However, despite decades of research, the specific molecular identities of these mechanosensitive ion channels in mammals have remained elusive. In this study, we set out to identify the proteins responsible for these currents. We characterized a rapidly adapting MA current in a mouse neuroblastoma cell line and utilized expression profiling combined with RNA interference (RNAi) knockdown to pinpoint candidate genes. Through this approach, we identified Piezo1, and its related family member Piezo2, as essential components of distinct MA cation channels.
