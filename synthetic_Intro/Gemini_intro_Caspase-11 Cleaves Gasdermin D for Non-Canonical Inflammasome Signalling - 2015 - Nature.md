The detection of intracellular lipopolysaccharide (LPS) from Gram-negative bacteria triggers a robust inflammatory response, critically mediated by the non-canonical inflammasome. This pathway activates mouse caspase-11 (and its human orthologues), leading to a rapid, lytic cell death termed pyroptosis, alongside the processing of interleukin-1β (IL-1β). These events are vital for host defense but can also drive lethal endotoxic shock. However, the direct molecular substrates of caspase-11 that execute these critical downstream functions remain unknown. Here, we utilize an unbiased forward genetic screen to identify gasdermin D as the essential target of caspase-11.
