# Introduction

Mechanotransduction converts physical force into biochemical and electrical signals and underlies diverse physiological processes, including touch, pain, hearing, vascular tone, and visceral sensation. Electrophysiological recordings have revealed mechanically-activated (MA) cation currents in many vertebrate cell types, but the molecules that mediate these currents have remained unknown. Several ion channels have been proposed to participate in mammalian mechanosensation, yet none has been shown to be generally required for native MA currents or sufficient to confer robust mechanically-evoked conductance in heterologous cells. We therefore sought to identify proteins necessary for MA channel activity by combining cellular electrophysiology, expression profiling, and RNA interference. Here we report that Piezo1 and Piezo2, two large multipass transmembrane proteins with broad evolutionary conservation, are essential components of distinct MA cation channels.
