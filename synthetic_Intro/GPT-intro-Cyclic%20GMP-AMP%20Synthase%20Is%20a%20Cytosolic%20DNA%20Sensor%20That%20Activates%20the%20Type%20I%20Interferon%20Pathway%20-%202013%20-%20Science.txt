# Introduction [114 words]

The detection of cytosolic DNA is an important mechanism by which mammalian cells mount innate immune responses against DNA viruses, bacteria, and parasites. DNA introduced into the cytoplasm triggers activation of the transcription factor IRF3 and induction of type I interferons through the adaptor protein STING, but how DNA is sensed upstream of STING has remained unclear. We recently showed that cytosolic DNA induces the production of cyclic GMP-AMP (cGAMP), a cyclic dinucleotide that binds to and activates STING. These results suggested the existence of a DNA-dependent enzyme that synthesizes cGAMP. Here, using biochemical purification and quantitative mass spectrometry, we identify this enzyme as cyclic GMP-AMP synthase (cGAS), and show that it functions as a cytosolic DNA sensor.
