Inflammasomes activate inflammatory caspases that mediate host defence and inflammatory disease. The canonical inflammasomes activate caspase-1, whereas cytoplasmic lipopolysaccharide from Gram-negative bacteria activates caspase-11 in mice and caspase-4 and caspase-5 in humans. Caspase-11 triggers pyroptotic cell death, NLRP3-dependent caspase-1 activation, interleukin-1β secretion and lethal endotoxic shock. Although caspase-11 directly senses intracellular lipopolysaccharide, the downstream substrate that couples caspase-11 activation to membrane rupture and cytokine maturation has remained unknown. Here, using forward genetics and biochemical analysis, we identify gasdermin D as this essential effector.
