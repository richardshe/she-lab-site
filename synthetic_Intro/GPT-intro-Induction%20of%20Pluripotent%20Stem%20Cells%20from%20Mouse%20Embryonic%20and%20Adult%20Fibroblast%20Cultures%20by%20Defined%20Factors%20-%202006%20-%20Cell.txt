# Introduction

Embryonic stem (ES) cells, which are derived from the inner cell mass of blastocysts, proliferate indefinitely in culture while maintaining the ability to give rise to all cell types of the body. These properties have made ES cells a useful system for analyzing early development and a potential source of differentiated cells for regenerative medicine. However, ES cells are obtained from preimplantation embryos, and the establishment of patient-specific pluripotent cells by this route remains technically and ethically difficult.

Somatic cells can acquire pluripotent properties after nuclear transfer into oocytes or after fusion with ES cells. These observations indicate that differentiated nuclei are not irreversibly fixed and that oocytes and ES cells contain activities capable of reprogramming somatic chromosomes to an embryonic state. Nevertheless, the molecular nature of these activities has remained largely unknown. Identification of defined factors that can induce pluripotency would provide a direct approach to studying the mechanisms of nuclear reprogramming and might allow the generation of pluripotent cells without using embryos or oocytes.

Several transcription factors have been implicated in the maintenance of ES cell identity. Oct3/4 is essential for the formation and maintenance of the pluripotent lineage, and changes in Oct3/4 expression levels induce differentiation. Sox2 and Nanog also have important roles in sustaining the undifferentiated state of ES cells. In addition, signaling molecules and transcriptional regulators associated with self-renewal, proliferation, and chromatin modification may contribute to the reprogramming activity present in ES cells.

We reasoned that factors required to maintain ES cell identity might also be sufficient, in appropriate combinations, to induce pluripotency in somatic cells. To test this possibility, we selected candidate genes preferentially expressed in ES cells or implicated in ES cell self-renewal and introduced them into mouse fibroblasts carrying a selectable reporter for activation of an ES cell-specific locus.
