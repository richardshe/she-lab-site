The heterochronic gene lin-4 was successfully cloned and found to encode two small RNAs, rather than a protein. These small RNAs possess sequence complementarity to the 3' UTR of lin-14 mRNA, suggesting a novel mechanism of post-transcriptional regulation via antisense RNA interaction. This antisense interaction likely mediates the critical temporal downregulation of LIN-14 protein required for normal postembryonic developmental progression in C. elegans. The identification of lin-4 as a non-coding, small regulatory RNA introduces a new paradigm for understanding temporal developmental switches and highlights the potential for small RNAs to act as precise, stage-specific translational regulators in diverse organisms.
