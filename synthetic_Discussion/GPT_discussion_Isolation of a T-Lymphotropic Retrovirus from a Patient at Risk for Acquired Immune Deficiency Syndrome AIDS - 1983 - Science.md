The present isolate extends the range of human T-lymphotropic retroviruses and shows that members of this family may be recovered from patients with lymphadenopathy preceding AIDS. Its growth in normal adult and cord blood lymphocytes, morphology, density, and reverse transcriptase activity establish its retroviral nature, whereas its lack of reaction with antisera to HTLV-I p19 and p24 indicates that it is not an ordinary HTLV-I isolate. The p25 antigen recognized by sera from two related patients suggests a specific immune response to this virus or to a closely related agent. Although these observations do not prove an etiologic role in AIDS, they support further serological and molecular studies in patients at risk and in established cases.
