# Discussion

Our structural determination of the active-state β2AR-Gs ternary complex provides the first high-resolution visualization of transmembrane signaling by a G protein-coupled receptor. The structure reveals that agonist binding induces a 14 Å outward movement of TM6 and the extension of TM5, creating an intracellular cavity that docks the α-helical extension of the Gαs subunit. Surprisingly, we observed a massive 127° displacement of the Gαs α-helical domain relative to its Ras-like GTPase domain. This dramatic domain separation explains the structural basis for GDP release and the stabilization of the nucleotide-free state required for G protein activation. The lack of direct interaction between the receptor and Gβγ subunits emphasizes the primary role of Gαs in driving receptor coupling. This landmark structure not only illuminates the mechanics of the ternary complex model but also establishes a fundamental structural framework for understanding GPCR-mediated signal transduction across the plasma membrane.
