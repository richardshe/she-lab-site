# Discussion

Our findings reveal that interactions among multivalent signaling proteins can drive macroscopic liquid-liquid phase separations, corresponding to sharp molecular sol-gel transitions. This phenomenon provides a mechanism by which Angstrom-scale protein interactions manifest as micrometer-scale cellular structures, such as signaling puncta. In the nephrin-NCK-N-WASP system, the phase transition directly couples to a dramatic, switch-like increase in Arp2/3-mediated actin nucleation activity. Importantly, this transition threshold is highly sensitive to the degree of nephrin phosphorylation, highlighting how kinase activity can tune the system’s physical state and functional output.

The formation of these dynamic, liquid-like droplets allows cells to locally concentrate specific biochemical components while excluding others, potentially enhancing reaction rates and sequestering signaling pathways. Because multivalent interactions—such as repeating SH3 domains, PRMs, and multiple phosphorylation sites—are ubiquitous across biological systems, liquid-liquid phase separation may be a fundamental and widespread principle for spatial organization. From receptor clustering at the plasma membrane to RNA processing in the nucleus, macroscopic phase transitions offer a versatile means for cells to compartmentalize information and exquisitely regulate biochemical activities in space and time.
