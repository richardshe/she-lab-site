The derivation of eleven patient-specific human embryonic stem cell lines via somatic cell nuclear transfer marks a critical advancement in the pursuit of regenerative therapies. By demonstrating efficient establishment on human feeders regardless of donor age or sex, we circumvent the persistent challenges of immune rejection that plague traditional transplantation. Furthermore, our modified protocol significantly mitigates previous low derivation rates while maintaining immunological compatibility. Despite these promising developments, the rigorous requirement for preclinical evaluation cannot be overstated. Future studies must definitively establish the epigenetic stability and directed differentiation capabilities of these NT-hESCs before any clinical application is realized. Moreover, the complete removal of all remaining animal-derived components during derivation remains an essential prerequisite for translation.
