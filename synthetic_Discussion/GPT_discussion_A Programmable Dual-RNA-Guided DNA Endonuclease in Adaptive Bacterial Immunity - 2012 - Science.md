Our results establish Cas9 as an RNA-guided endonuclease whose activity is specified by a dual-RNA structure rather than by crRNA alone. In this system, tracrRNA performs a second function beyond pre-crRNA maturation: it enables Cas9 to bind target DNA and position the crRNA guide for sequence-specific recognition. DNA cleavage is executed by two distinct nuclease domains within Cas9, with the HNH domain cutting the strand complementary to the crRNA and the RuvC-like domain cutting the opposite strand to generate a double-stranded break near the PAM. Targeting is further constrained by PAM recognition and by a seed region proximal to the PAM, providing a mechanism for efficient discrimination of cognate protospacers and escape variants. The ability to replace the natural tracrRNA:crRNA pair with a single chimeric RNA demonstrates that the essential targeting information can be encoded in a compact programmable guide. Thus, Type II CRISPR/Cas systems define a simple class of RNA-directed DNA endonucleases with broad potential for site-specific genome manipulation.
