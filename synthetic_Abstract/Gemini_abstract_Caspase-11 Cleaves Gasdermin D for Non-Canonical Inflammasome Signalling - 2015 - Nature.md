The non-canonical inflammasome, activated by cytoplasmic lipopolysaccharide (LPS), triggers pyroptosis and interleukin-1β (IL-1β) secretion to defend against Gram-negative bacteria, but the precise mechanism of caspase-11-mediated cell death has remained elusive. Through an unbiased ENU-mutagenesis screen in mice, we identified gasdermin D (Gsdmd) as a crucial mediator of non-canonical inflammasome signalling. Macrophages from Gsdmd-deficient mice exhibit defective pyroptosis and fail to secrete IL-1β in response to cytoplasmic LPS. We demonstrate that inflammatory caspases (caspase-11 in mice, and caspases-4 and -5 in humans) directly cleave Gsdmd. This proteolytic cleavage generates an N-terminal fragment that is sufficient to induce pyroptosis and is also required for the subsequent activation of the NLRP3-dependent canonical inflammasome. Moreover, Gsdmd-deficient mice are protected from LPS-induced lethal endotoxemia. Our findings reveal that Gsdmd is an essential substrate of inflammatory caspases, executing pyroptosis and driving innate immune responses against intracellular bacteria.
