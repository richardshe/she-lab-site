Genetic analysis has localized the major familial breast and ovarian cancer susceptibility locus BRCA1 to chromosome 17q21. We identified a transcription unit within the minimal interval between D17S1321 and D17S1325 that encodes a predicted 1863-amino acid protein containing an NH2-terminal C3HC4 zinc finger motif. The gene spans approximately 100 kb, is composed of 22 coding exons, and produces a 7.8-kb transcript expressed in breast, ovary, and several other tissues. Sequence analysis of affected haplotype carriers from BRCA1-linked kindreds revealed frameshift, nonsense, missense, and probable regulatory mutations. These alterations cosegregated with the predisposing chromosome and were absent from appropriate control populations. The nature of the mutations, together with the genetic localization and expression pattern of the transcript, strongly indicates that this gene is BRCA1, a likely tumor suppressor whose mutation predisposes to early-onset breast cancer and ovarian cancer.
