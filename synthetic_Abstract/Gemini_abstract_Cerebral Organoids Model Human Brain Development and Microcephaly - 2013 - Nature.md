The unique complexity and expansion of the human brain compared to other mammals make it challenging to model human neurodevelopmental disorders using traditional animal models. Here, we present a 3D in vitro culture system that derives complex brain tissues, termed cerebral organoids, from human pluripotent stem cells. By utilizing an optimized protocol promoting intrinsic developmental cues, these organoids rapidly self-organize into interdependent, heterogeneous brain regions including the cerebral cortex, choroid plexus, and retina. The cerebral cortical tissues display typical inside-out layered organization and an outer radial glia (oRG) population akin to the human outer subventricular zone. Using patient-derived induced pluripotent stem cells, we modeled microcephaly caused by CDK5RAP2 mutations, revealing premature neural differentiation and defective radial glial proliferation. Cerebral organoids therefore offer a powerful platform to study human-specific mechanisms of neurodevelopment and the pathogenesis of complex neurological diseases.
