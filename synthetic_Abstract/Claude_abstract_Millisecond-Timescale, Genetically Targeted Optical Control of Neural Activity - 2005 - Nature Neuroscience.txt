Temporally precise, noninvasive control of activity in well-defined neuronal populations is a long-sought goal of systems neuroscience. We demonstrate here that the single-component light-gated cation channel Channelrhodopsin-2 (ChR2), when lentivirally expressed in cultured hippocampal neurons, mediates large, rapidly activating photocurrents that reliably drive single action potentials at millisecond timescales using only brief pulses of blue light. ChR2-expressing neurons faithfully follow trains of light pulses spanning physiologically relevant frequencies and reproduce arbitrary, Poisson-distributed spike patterns with low jitter both within and across cells, without compromising membrane integrity, basal electrical properties or survival, even after prolonged photostimulation.
