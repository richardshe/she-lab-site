The topography of the rII region of phage T4, that is, the local differences in the mutational properties of its parts, has been examined by mapping some thousands of reverting mutants. By making use of overlapping deletions, the structure has been carved into 47 ordered segments, within which point mutations are then placed against one another by simple qualitative spot tests for recombination. Over 2,400 spontaneous and induced mutations, occupying more than 300 distinct sites, have been located. The distribution is decidedly non-random: at the most prominent hotspot more than 500 occurrences have been observed, while at many sites only a single occurrence, or none, has been found. Each mutagen yields a characteristic spectrum, and the sites are seen to fall into many classes that cannot be accounted for by base pair composition alone.
