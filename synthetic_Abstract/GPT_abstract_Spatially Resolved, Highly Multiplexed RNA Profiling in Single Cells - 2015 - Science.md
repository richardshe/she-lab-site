Single-molecule fluorescence in situ hybridization allows quantitative imaging of RNAs in individual cells but has been limited in multiplexing capacity. Here we report MERFISH, a multiplexed error-robust fluorescence in situ hybridization method that combines combinatorial labeling, sequential imaging, and Hamming-distance-based encoding schemes to profile RNAs in single cells. We used MERFISH to measure 140 RNA species in human fibroblasts with an error-correcting 16-bit code and 1001 RNA species with an error-detecting 14-bit code. The resulting RNA copy numbers agreed well with conventional smFISH and bulk RNA sequencing measurements. Analysis of cell-to-cell variation revealed groups of co-regulated genes, whereas spatial correlation analysis identified distinct subcellular RNA localization patterns. These results establish MERFISH as a highly multiplexed imaging approach for in situ transcriptomic analysis.
