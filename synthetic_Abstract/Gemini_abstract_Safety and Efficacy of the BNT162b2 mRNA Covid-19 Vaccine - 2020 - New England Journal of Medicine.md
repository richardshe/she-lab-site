The severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) pandemic necessitates the rapid development of a safe and effective vaccine to mitigate the profound global medical and economic impacts. We report the safety and efficacy results from the phase 2/3 portion of a global, phase 1/2/3, observer-blinded, placebo-controlled trial evaluating BNT162b2, a lipid nanoparticle-formulated, nucleoside-modified RNA vaccine encoding the prefusion-stabilized full-length spike protein of SARS-CoV-2. A total of 43,548 individuals aged 16 years or older were randomized to receive two doses of either 30 μg of BNT162b2 or placebo, administered intramuscularly 21 days apart. The primary endpoints were the safety of the vaccine and its efficacy in preventing coronavirus disease 2019 (Covid-19) with onset at least 7 days after the second dose in participants with and without prior evidence of SARS-CoV-2 infection. BNT162b2 was highly effective, demonstrating a 95% efficacy against Covid-19 (95% credible interval, 90.3 to 97.6). Among participants without evidence of prior infection, 8 cases of Covid-19 were observed in the vaccine group compared to 162 in the placebo group. The vaccine's efficacy was consistent across age, sex, race, ethnicity, and coexisting condition subgroups. The safety profile of BNT162b2 was favorable and characterized primarily by mild-to-moderate, transient local reactogenicity, such as injection-site pain, and systemic reactogenicity events, including fatigue, headache, and fever, which were more frequent after the second dose and in younger recipients. Severe adverse events and serious adverse events were rare and comparable to the placebo group. In conclusion, the two-dose regimen of the BNT162b2 mRNA vaccine confers robust protection against Covid-19 and exhibits a safety profile comparable to that of other viral vaccines, establishing it as a critical tool in managing the ongoing pandemic.
