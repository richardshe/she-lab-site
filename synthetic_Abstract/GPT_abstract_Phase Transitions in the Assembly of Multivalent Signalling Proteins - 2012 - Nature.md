Multivalent interactions are common in signalling proteins, but their capacity to drive sharp changes in molecular organization and activity has been unclear. Here we show that weak, modular interactions between synthetic SH3-domain and proline-rich-motif polymers produce concentration- and valency-dependent liquid-liquid phase separation. The dense phase contains both components at approximately equal stoichiometry and at concentrations about 100-fold higher than the surrounding solution, and displays properties consistent with a polymeric gel. Dynamic and small-angle X-ray scattering, fluorescence recovery after photobleaching and cryo-electron microscopy indicate that droplet formation is driven by assembly into large, disordered, crosslinked species, in a transition coupled to sol-gel polymerization. Similar puncta form in cells when multivalent SH3 and ligand arrays are coexpressed. We further show that the natural nephrin-NCK-N-WASP signalling system undergoes analogous phosphorylation-regulated phase transitions, and that entry into the droplet/polymer state sharply enhances Arp2/3-mediated actin assembly. These findings suggest that multivalent proteins can generate switch-like transitions between dispersed and condensed states, providing a physical mechanism for organizing cellular bodies and producing nonlinear signalling responses.
