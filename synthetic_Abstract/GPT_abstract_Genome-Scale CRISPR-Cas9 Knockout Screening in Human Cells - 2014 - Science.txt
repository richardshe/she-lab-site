Systematic loss-of-function screening in mammalian cells has relied primarily on RNA interference, but incomplete knockdown and off-target effects limit its precision. Here we describe genome-scale CRISPR-Cas9 knockout (GeCKO) screening using a single lentiviral vector that delivers Cas9, a single-guide RNA, and a selectable marker. In cells carrying a single-copy EGFP reporter, lentiCRISPR mediated highly efficient target disruption, producing loss of fluorescence in 93% of cells and indel formation at the target locus. We constructed a pooled library targeting 18,080 human genes and showed that negative selection in melanoma and human stem cells depleted guides against essential genes. In a positive selection screen for resistance to the BRAF inhibitor vemurafenib, GeCKO identified known mediators, including NF1 and MED12, and additional candidates such as NF2, CUL3, TADA1, and TADA2B. Individual validation confirmed robust mutagenesis and drug resistance. These results establish pooled Cas9:sgRNA screening as a powerful platform for functional genomics.
