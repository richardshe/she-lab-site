DNA is a potent immune stimulant, but the mechanism by which cytosolic DNA activates type I interferons has remained unclear. We purified the cyclic GMP-AMP synthase activity from murine cytosolic extracts by combining conventional chromatography, DNA affinity purification, and quantitative mass spectrometry, and identified E330016A19, the mouse homolog of human C6orf150/MB21D1, as cGAS. Mouse and human cGAS bound DNA and catalyzed the synthesis of cyclic GMP-AMP (cGAMP) from ATP and GTP in a DNA-dependent manner. Catalytic mutations abolished cGAMP production and interferon-beta induction. Depletion of cGAS in mouse L929 cells or human THP-1 cells impaired IRF3 activation, cGAMP synthesis, and interferon-beta induction by transfected DNA or DNA viruses, but not by an RNA virus. Thus, cGAS is a cytosolic DNA sensor that activates STING through the second messenger cGAMP.
