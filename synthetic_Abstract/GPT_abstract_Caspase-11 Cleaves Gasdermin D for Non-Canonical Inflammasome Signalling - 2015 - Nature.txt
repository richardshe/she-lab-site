Cytoplasmic lipopolysaccharide (LPS) activates caspase-11 to induce pyroptotic cell death and NLRP3-dependent caspase-1 activation, but the substrate that executes these responses has remained unknown. Here we identify gasdermin D (Gsdmd) as an essential mediator of non-canonical inflammasome signalling. An ENU mutagenesis screen uncovered a damaging Gsdmd mutation that selectively abolished LPS-induced interleukin-1beta secretion and pyroptosis while leaving canonical inflammasome responses intact. Gene-targeted Gsdmd-deficient macrophages phenocopied Casp11 deficiency after cytoplasmic LPS delivery or Gram-negative bacterial infection, and human GSDMD was similarly required for caspase-4-dependent LPS cytotoxicity. Caspase-11 directly cleaved Gsdmd at Asp276 to generate an amino-terminal 30-kDa fragment whose expression was sufficient to kill cells. Mutation of this cleavage site prevented LPS-induced pyroptosis, caspase-1 processing and interleukin-1beta release. Gsdmd-deficient mice were also resistant to LPS-induced lethal septic shock. Thus gasdermin D is a conserved, critical caspase substrate that couples cytoplasmic LPS sensing to inflammatory cell death and sepsis.
