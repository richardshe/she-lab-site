# Abstract

Precision genome engineering requires scalable and efficient tools. Here, we repurpose the RNA-guided Cas9 nuclease from the Streptococcus pyogenes type II CRISPR adaptive immune system to achieve targeted, highly efficient genome editing in mammalian cells. By codelivering a codon-optimized Cas9 and customized chimeric guide RNAs, we demonstrate the rapid creation of double-stranded breaks and precision modifications at multiple endogenous genomic loci in both human and mouse cells. We also engineer a mutant Cas9 nickase to facilitate high-fidelity homology-directed repair with minimal off-target effects. Furthermore, the innate array architecture of the CRISPR system enables multiplexed editing, allowing simultaneous perturbation of multiple genes. This versatile, RNA-programmable technology provides a robust platform for functional genomics, basic biology, and potential therapeutic applications.
