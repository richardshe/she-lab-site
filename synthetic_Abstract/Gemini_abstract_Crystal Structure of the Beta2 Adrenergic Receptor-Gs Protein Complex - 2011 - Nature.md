G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) are ubiquitous membrane proteins responsible for transducing extracellular signals into intracellular responses via heterotrimeric G proteins. Despite significant progress in structural studies of inactive GPCRs, the structural basis for the active state and GPCR-G protein coupling has remained elusive. Here we present the 3.2 Å crystal structure of the human β2 adrenergic receptor (β2AR) in complex with the stimulatory G protein, Gs. To stabilize this highly dynamic complex for crystallogenesis, we utilized a high-affinity agonist, a customized detergent, T4 lysozyme fusion, and an application of a camelid nanobody (Nb35) that binds the G protein heterotrimer interface. The active-state β2AR exhibits a large 14 Å outward movement of transmembrane helix 6 (TM6) compared to its inactive conformation, creating an intracellular cavity that docks the Gαs Ras-like domain. Strikingly, the α-helical domain of Gαs undergoes a massive displacement relative to the Ras-like domain, revealing the mechanism of GDP release and nucleotide exchange. This structure provides the first high-resolution view of transmembrane signaling by a GPCR, offering critical insights into the mechanism of receptor-mediated G protein activation.
