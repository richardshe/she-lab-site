Mechanically-activated cation currents have been described in many cell types, but their molecular identity in vertebrates has remained unclear. We screened candidate membrane proteins enriched in Neuro2A cells, a cell line expressing robust mechanically-activated currents, and found that knockdown of Fam38A, here named Piezo1, strongly reduced these currents. Heterologous expression of Piezo1 in several cell lines conferred large pressure-evoked, non-selective cation currents that were blocked by gadolinium and ruthenium red. A related protein, Piezo2, was highly expressed in dorsal root ganglion neurons and also generated mechanically-activated cation currents when expressed in heterologous cells. Piezo2-dependent currents inactivated more rapidly than Piezo1 currents, and Piezo2 knockdown selectively reduced rapidly adapting mechanically-activated currents in sensory neurons. Piezos therefore define essential components of distinct mechanically-activated cation channels with broad roles in mechanotransduction.
