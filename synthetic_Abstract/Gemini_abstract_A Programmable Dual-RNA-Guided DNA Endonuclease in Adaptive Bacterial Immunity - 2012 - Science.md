The CRISPR/Cas system represents an adaptive immune mechanism in bacteria and archaea that protects against invading viruses and plasmids. In Type II CRISPR/Cas systems, the Cas9 endonuclease cleaves target DNA through an RNA-guided process. Here, we demonstrate that Cas9 target recognition and cleavage require a dual-RNA structure consisting of mature CRISPR RNA (crRNA) and trans-activating crRNA (tracrRNA). The tracrRNA:crRNA complex directs Cas9 to introduce site-specific double-stranded breaks in target DNA, with the HNH and RuvC-like nuclease domains cleaving the complementary and non-complementary strands, respectively. DNA target recognition requires both a seed sequence in the crRNA and a specific protospacer adjacent motif (PAM). Furthermore, we show that Cas9 can be programmed using a single engineered chimeric guide RNA transcript, offering a highly efficient, versatile, and easily programmable platform for targeted DNA cleavage and genome editing.
