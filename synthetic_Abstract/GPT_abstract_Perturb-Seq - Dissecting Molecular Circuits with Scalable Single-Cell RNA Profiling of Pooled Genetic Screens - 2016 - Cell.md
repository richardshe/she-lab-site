Pooled genetic screens are powerful but have been largely restricted to low-content phenotypes, whereas transcriptional profiling of perturbations has remained difficult to scale. We developed Perturb-seq, which couples CRISPR/Cas9-mediated perturbations with massively parallel single-cell RNA sequencing by encoding each single-guide RNA with an expressed guide barcode. This strategy links perturbation identity to whole-transcriptome profiles in individual cells and can be tuned to assay single genes or combinations. We applied Perturb-seq to primary bone marrow-derived dendritic cells responding to lipopolysaccharide and to proliferating K562 cells, analyzing approximately 200,000 cells across six experiments. Using MIMOSCA, a Multi-Input-Multi-Output-Single-Cell-Analysis framework, we inferred effects of individual perturbations and genetic interactions on genes, programs, and cell states. Perturb-seq recovered regulatory modules, transcription factor-specific functions, and distinct mechanisms underlying related fitness phenotypes, providing a scalable framework for dissecting molecular circuits with rich genomic readouts.
