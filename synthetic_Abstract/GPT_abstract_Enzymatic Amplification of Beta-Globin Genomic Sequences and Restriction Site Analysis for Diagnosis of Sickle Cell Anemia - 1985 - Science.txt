We describe a rapid and sensitive procedure for diagnosing sickle cell anemia by direct analysis of genomic DNA. A 110-base pair segment of the beta-globin gene spanning the polymorphic Dde I site altered by the beta^S mutation was amplified by repeated cycles of primer-directed DNA synthesis. The amplified product was then examined by solution hybridization with a labeled oligomer and sequential digestion with Dde I and Hinf I, producing allele-specific cleavage fragments. The method correctly distinguished AA, AS, and SS samples, required as little as 20 nanograms of DNA, and permitted beta-globin genotyping in less than 10 hours.
