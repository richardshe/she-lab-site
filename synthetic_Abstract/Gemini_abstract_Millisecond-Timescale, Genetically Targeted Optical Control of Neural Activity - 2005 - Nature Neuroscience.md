# Abstract

Understanding neural computation requires manipulating specific neurons with high temporal precision. We present a genetically encoded strategy for millisecond-timescale optical control of neural activity using Channelrhodopsin-2 (ChR2), a light-gated cation channel from Chlamydomonas reinhardtii. Expressed in mammalian neurons, ChR2 mediates rapid depolarizing currents upon blue light illumination. Using brief light pulses, we reliably generated precise, naturalistic spike trains and synaptic events with submillisecond jitter. ChR2 expression does not alter basal electrical properties or cell health. This monolithic optical switch enables genetically targeted, temporally precise remote control of neural circuits, offering a powerful tool for dissecting the building blocks of information processing.
