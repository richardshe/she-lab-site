Current methods for mapping chromatin accessibility and transcription factor occupancy are powerful but require millions of cells and involve complex, lengthy protocols, limiting their application to rare cell populations and clinical samples. Here, we introduce Assay for Transposase-Accessible Chromatin using sequencing (ATAC-seq), a rapid and highly sensitive method for epigenomic profiling. ATAC-seq utilizes a hyperactive Tn5 transposase to simultaneously cleave and tag accessible regions of the genome with sequencing adaptors. We demonstrate that ATAC-seq captures the interplay between open chromatin, nucleosome positioning, and transcription factor footprints using as few as 500 cells. Furthermore, we illustrate its clinical feasibility by successfully generating personalized regulatory maps and identifying allele-specific chromatin states from standard human blood draws within hours. ATAC-seq provides a robust, fast, and sensitive tool for multidimensional epigenomic analysis.
