We have isolated the mouse obese (ob) gene by positional cloning and identified its human homologue. The gene encodes a 4.5-kb messenger RNA expressed principally in adipose tissue and predicts a conserved 167-amino-acid protein with an amino-terminal signal sequence. The original C57BL/6J ob mutation is a nonsense change at codon 105 and is associated with increased ob RNA, whereas the SM/Ckc-Dac ob2J allele lacks detectable transcript. The conservation and probable secretion of ob suggest an adipose-derived signal regulating body fat stores.
