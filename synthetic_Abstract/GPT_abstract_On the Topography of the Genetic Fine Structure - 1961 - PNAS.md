The fine structure of the rII region of phage T4 has been examined with respect to its topography, that is, the local differences in mutability among its parts. By means of overlapping deletions, thousands of reverting mutants have been assigned by qualitative recombination tests to a linear series of 47 segments, and distinct mutable sites have then been recognized within these segments. Spontaneous mutations are distributed in a markedly non-random fashion. A few sites recur with very high frequency, whereas many others are represented only once or remain undetected; the observed 1,612 spontaneous mutants define 251 sites and imply many additional sites. Mutations induced by several chemical agents reveal further sites and characteristic spectra, bringing the total mapped to over 300. The results show that genetic sites differ widely in spontaneous mutability and in response to mutagens, and suggest a highly differentiated nucleotide organization within the cistron.
