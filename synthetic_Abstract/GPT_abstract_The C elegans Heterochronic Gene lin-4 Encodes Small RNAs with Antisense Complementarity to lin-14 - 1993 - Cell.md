The C. elegans heterochronic gene lin-4 is required for the normal temporal decrease in lin-14 activity that permits the transition from L1-specific to later larval developmental programs. We cloned lin-4 by chromosomal walking and transformation rescue and localized rescuing activity to a 693 bp genomic fragment. Sequence comparisons among four Caenorhabditis species and mutational analysis of predicted open reading frames indicate that lin-4 is unlikely to encode a protein. Instead, lin-4 produces two small RNAs, approximately 61 and 22 nt in length, that are absent from a lin-4 deletion mutant and affected by a newly isolated lin-4 loss-of-function point mutation. The lin-4 RNAs contain sequences complementary to an element repeated seven times in the 3' untranslated region of lin-14 mRNA, the region required for lin-4-dependent repression of LIN-14 protein accumulation. These results suggest that lin-4 regulates developmental timing by producing small RNAs that inhibit lin-14 translation through an antisense interaction with its 3' untranslated region.
