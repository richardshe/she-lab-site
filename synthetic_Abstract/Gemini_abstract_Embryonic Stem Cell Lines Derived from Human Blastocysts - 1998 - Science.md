Human embryonic stem (ES) cell lines were derived from preimplantation blastocysts produced by in vitro fertilization. Five independent lines were established, demonstrating prolonged undifferentiated proliferation and normal karyotypes. These cells exhibit high telomerase activity and express cell surface markers characteristic of primate ES cells. When injected into immunodeficient mice, the cells form teratomas containing derivatives of all three embryonic germ layers: endoderm, mesoderm, and ectoderm. They also demonstrate the capacity for spontaneous in vitro differentiation. These pluripotent human ES cell lines provide a powerful model for understanding early human development, discovering new drug targets, and developing future tissue regeneration therapies.
