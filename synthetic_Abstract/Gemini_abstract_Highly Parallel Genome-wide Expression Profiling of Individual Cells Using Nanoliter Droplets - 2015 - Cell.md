Complex tissues consist of numerous cell types and states, yet our understanding of cellular diversity remains incomplete because scalable methods for single-cell transcriptional profiling are lacking. Here, we present Drop-seq, a highly scalable technique for analyzing mRNA expression in thousands of individual cells. Drop-seq uses a microfluidic device to encapsulate single cells together with distinctly barcoded microparticles in nanoliter-scale aqueous droplets. Within these droplets, cells are lysed, and their mRNAs are captured on the microparticles, establishing a molecular memory of the cell of origin using unique barcodes generated through a split-and-pool synthesis strategy. We demonstrated the accuracy and efficiency of Drop-seq by analyzing mixtures of human and mouse cells. We then applied Drop-seq to profile 44,808 cells from the mouse retina, successfully identifying 39 distinct, biologically meaningful cell populations. Drop-seq enables massive, low-cost single-cell transcriptomics, providing a transformative tool for mapping cell types and states in complex biological systems.
