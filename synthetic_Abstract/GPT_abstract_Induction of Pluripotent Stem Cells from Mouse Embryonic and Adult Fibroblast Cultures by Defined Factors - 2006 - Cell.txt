Embryonic stem (ES) cells can proliferate indefinitely while retaining the ability to differentiate into derivatives of all three germ layers. Somatic nuclei can be reprogrammed by oocytes or by fusion with ES cells, but the defined factors sufficient for inducing pluripotency have remained unclear. We screened 24 ES cell-associated genes using activation of an Fbx15-bgeo reporter as a selectable indicator of reprogramming in mouse fibroblasts. Introduction of all candidates generated ES cell-like colonies, and sequential withdrawal experiments identified four critical factors: Oct3/4, Sox2, c-Myc, and Klf4. These factors induced cells from mouse embryonic fibroblasts and adult tail-tip fibroblasts that expressed many ES cell markers, showed ES cell-like morphology and proliferation, and exhibited epigenetic changes at pluripotency-associated loci. The induced pluripotent stem cells differentiated into tissues of all three germ layers in vitro and in teratomas, and contributed to embryonic development after blastocyst injection. Thus, pluripotent cells can be generated directly from fibroblast cultures by defined factors.
