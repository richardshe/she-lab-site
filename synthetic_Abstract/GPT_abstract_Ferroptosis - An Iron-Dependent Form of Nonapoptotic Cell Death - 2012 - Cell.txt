Nonapoptotic cell death pathways are increasingly recognized as regulated processes with distinct developmental and pathological functions. Here we describe ferroptosis, an iron-dependent form of cell death triggered in oncogenic RAS mutant tumor cells by the RAS-selective lethal compounds erastin and RSL3. Ferroptosis was accompanied by sustained cytosolic and lipid reactive oxygen species accumulation and was suppressed by iron chelation and antioxidants, but not by inhibitors of apoptosis, necroptosis, autophagy, or other known death programs. Genetic screens identified RPL8, IREB2, ATP5G3, CS, TTC35, and ACSF2 as specific mediators of erastin-induced ferroptosis, implicating iron regulation, translation, and lipid metabolism. We further identified ferrostatin-1, a potent small-molecule inhibitor that blocks lipid ROS accumulation and protects both cancer cells and organotypic hippocampal slice cultures from glutamate-induced toxicity. Erastin inhibits cystine uptake by system xc-, thereby weakening antioxidant defenses and enabling iron-dependent lethal oxidation. These findings define ferroptosis as a distinct regulated death mechanism with potential relevance to cancer therapy and neuroprotection.
