Genetic screens have transformed our ability to dissect gene function, yet pooled formats have been restricted to low-content phenotypes, leaving mechanistic interpretation laborious. Here, we present Perturb-seq, which couples the modularity of CRISPR/Cas9 multi-locus perturbation with the rich genomic readout of massively parallel single-cell RNA sequencing. By encoding the identity of each guide on an expressed barcode, we associate every cell's transcriptome with its delivered perturbation, and by tuning the multiplicity of infection, we capture single-gene effects as well as higher-order genetic interactions. We profile approximately 200,000 cells across six experiments, targeting transcription factors and cell-cycle regulators in bone marrow-derived dendritic cells and K562 cells. A regularized linear framework, MIMOSCA, deconvolves the contribution of each perturbation to individual transcripts, gene programs, and cell states, recovering known regulators, opposing differentiation modules wired by feedback loops, and non-additive epistatic interactions supported by TF co-binding.
