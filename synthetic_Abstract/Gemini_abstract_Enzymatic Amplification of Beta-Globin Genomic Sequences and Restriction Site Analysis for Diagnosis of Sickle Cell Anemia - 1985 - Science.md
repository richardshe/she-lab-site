We have developed a rapid, highly sensitive method for the direct detection of the sickle cell mutation in human genomic DNA. The procedure involves specific enzymatic amplification of a beta-globin sequence using the polymerase chain reaction (PCR), followed by analysis via solution hybridization with a labeled oligonucleotide probe and restriction endonuclease digestion. This oligomer restriction method readily distinguishes the normal beta^A and mutant beta^S alleles. Our approach increases the sensitivity of prenatal diagnosis by over two orders of magnitude compared to standard Southern blotting. It is faster, simpler, and requires significantly less DNA, providing a powerful general approach for the diagnosis of genetic and infectious diseases.
