During Caenorhabditis elegans development, the temporal coordination of diverse cell lineages is governed by heterochronic genes. The gene lin-4 acts as a critical negative regulator of lin-14, facilitating the progression from early to late larval developmental programs by downregulating LIN-14 protein levels. Here, we report the molecular cloning and characterization of the lin-4 locus. Strikingly, our analysis reveals that lin-4 does not encode a protein. Instead, it produces two small RNA transcripts of approximately 22 and 61 nucleotides. Sequence comparison demonstrates that these small lin-4 RNAs contain regions of antisense complementarity to a repeated sequence element located within the 3' untranslated region (UTR) of the lin-14 messenger RNA. We propose a novel regulatory mechanism wherein lin-4 small RNAs directly hybridize to the lin-14 3' UTR, leading to the post-transcriptional inhibition of LIN-14 protein synthesis. These findings unveil a mechanism of developmental timing control mediated by small, non-coding antisense RNAs, highlighting a new paradigm in post-transcriptional gene regulation.
