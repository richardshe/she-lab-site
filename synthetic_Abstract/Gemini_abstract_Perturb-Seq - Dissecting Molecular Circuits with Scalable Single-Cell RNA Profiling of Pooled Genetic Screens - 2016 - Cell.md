# Abstract

Genetic screens are powerful tools for systematically elucidating gene function, but pooled screens have largely been restricted to low-dimensional readouts like cell viability. Understanding complex molecular circuits demands higher-content phenotyping. Here, we introduce Perturb-seq, a scalable approach combining combinatorial CRISPR/Cas9 perturbations with massively parallel single-cell RNA sequencing. By encoding perturbation identities on expressed guide barcodes, we can assay rich transcriptional phenotypes for many genetic targets simultaneously within a pooled format. We developed a computational framework, MIMOSCA, to deconvolve the effects of individual and combinatorial perturbations on specific genes, regulatory programs, and global cell states, while accounting for single-cell variability and incomplete perturbation efficacy. Applying Perturb-seq to immune and cancer cells, we successfully dissected complex transcription factor networks, unraveled regulatory mechanisms underlying cell differentiation and the anti-viral response, and explored genetic interactions. This modular platform dramatically enhances the resolution of pooled genetic screens.
