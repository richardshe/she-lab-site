Genetic susceptibility accounts for a significant proportion of early-onset breast and ovarian cancers. Linkage analysis has previously localized the primary susceptibility locus, BRCA1, to a narrow region on chromosome 17q21. We have employed a positional cloning strategy to identify a strong candidate for the BRCA1 gene. A detailed transcriptional map of the region yielded a single gene composed of 22 coding exons spanning roughly 100 kilobases of genomic DNA, which encodes a large 1863-amino acid protein featuring a zinc-finger domain. Mutational analysis of individuals from early-onset breast and ovarian cancer kindreds revealed frameshift, nonsense, missense, and likely regulatory mutations in this gene. These mutations cosegregate with the predisposing BRCA1 haplotype and are absent in control populations. Our data strongly suggest that this gene represents the BRCA1 breast and ovarian cancer susceptibility allele.
