G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) regulate a broad range of physiological processes by coupling extracellular agonist binding to activation of heterotrimeric G proteins. The β2 adrenergic receptor (β2AR) has long served as a model for understanding this process, but the structural basis for nucleotide exchange and signal transduction has remained unclear. Here we describe the crystal structure of the agonist-bound β2AR in complex with nucleotide-free Gs, determined at 3.2 Å resolution. Crystallization was enabled by stabilization of the complex in maltose neopentyl glycol detergent, fusion of T4 lysozyme to the receptor amino terminus, lipidic cubic phase crystallization, and a camelid nanobody that bridges the Gαs and Gβ subunits. The structure reveals a large outward displacement of receptor transmembrane helix 6 and extension of helix 5, forming an extensive interface with the Ras-like domain of Gαs. The carboxy-terminal α5 helix of Gαs inserts into the activated receptor core, while the Gαs α-helical domain undergoes a pronounced rotation away from the nucleotide-binding pocket. These observations provide a structural framework for GPCR-mediated G protein activation and suggest new opportunities for targeting receptor-G protein interfaces.
