Previous attempts to establish cultures of pluripotential cells directly from mouse embryos have been unsuccessful. We report here a method for deriving such cells from blastocysts maintained in implantational delay and subsequently cultured on inactivated STO fibroblast feeders. The inner cell mass gave rise to colonies of rapidly proliferating cells closely resembling embryonal carcinoma cells in morphology and growth requirements. These embryo-derived EK cells retained normal 40XX or 40XY karyotypes, excluding contamination by established embryonal carcinoma lines, and have been maintained for more than 30 passages. After injection into syngeneic mice they formed teratocarcinomas, and in vitro they generated embryoid bodies and differentiated tissues. These cultures provide a direct link between the early embryo and pluripotential cells in culture.
