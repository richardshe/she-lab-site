Background
Safe and effective vaccines are needed urgently to reduce coronavirus disease 2019 (Covid-19), caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). BNT162b2 is a lipid nanoparticle-formulated, nucleoside-modified RNA vaccine encoding the SARS-CoV-2 full-length prefusion spike protein.

Methods
In an ongoing multinational, placebo-controlled, observer-blinded, pivotal efficacy trial, we randomly assigned persons 16 years of age or older in a 1:1 ratio to receive two 30-μg doses of BNT162b2 or saline placebo, administered intramuscularly 21 days apart. The primary efficacy end points were prevention of confirmed Covid-19 with onset at least 7 days after the second dose among participants without previous evidence of SARS-CoV-2 infection and among all participants regardless of previous infection. Safety end points included solicited local and systemic reactions, adverse events, serious adverse events, and deaths.

Results
A total of 43,448 participants received injections: 21,720 received BNT162b2 and 21,728 received placebo. Among 36,523 participants without evidence of previous SARS-CoV-2 infection, 8 cases of Covid-19 with onset at least 7 days after the second dose occurred in vaccine recipients and 162 in placebo recipients, corresponding to vaccine efficacy of 95.0% (95% confidence interval, 90.3 to 97.6). Among participants with or without evidence of previous infection, vaccine efficacy was 94.6% (95% confidence interval, 89.9 to 97.3). Efficacy was generally consistent across subgroups defined by age, sex, race, ethnicity, obesity, and coexisting conditions. Reactogenicity was more common after BNT162b2 than after placebo and was generally mild or moderate and transient. Serious adverse events were uncommon and occurred with similar frequency in the two groups.

Conclusions
A two-dose regimen of BNT162b2 conferred 95% protection against Covid-19 in persons 16 years of age or older. Safety over a median of 2 months was characterized mainly by short-term local and systemic reactions.
