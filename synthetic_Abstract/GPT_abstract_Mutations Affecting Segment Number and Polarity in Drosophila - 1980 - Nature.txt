We have undertaken a systematic search for zygotic mutations affecting the segmental pattern of the Drosophila larva. Mutations at 15 loci define three classes of defects. Segment polarity mutants delete part of each segment and duplicate the remainder in reversed polarity. Pair-rule mutants delete homologous regions in alternating segments, implying a transient double-segmental organization. Gap mutants remove continuous groups of adjacent segments. The phenotypes indicate that embryonic segmentation is built by successive spatial organizations controlled by a small set of zygotic genes.
