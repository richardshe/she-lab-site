Pluripotential cell lines have been established directly from mouse blastocysts. Embryos of the 129 SvE strain were placed into implantational delay by ovariectomy and Depo-Provera treatment, recovered after 4-6 days, and cultured intact on tissue culture plastic. The resulting egg cylinder-like structures were dispersed and passaged on mitomycin C-inactivated STO feeder layers, where actively proliferating colonies closely resembling embryonal carcinoma (EC) cells appeared. These embryo-derived (EK) cells have a normal diploid karyotype, form teratocarcinomas when injected into syngeneic mice, differentiate into a complex of tissues via embryoid bodies, and share cell-surface antigens and protein synthesis patterns with EC cells. Fifteen independent lines have been obtained from 129 and outbred stocks.
