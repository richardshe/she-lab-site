In Drosophila the segmental pattern of the larva is established by the zygotic genome at the blastoderm stage. In a systematic search for mutations affecting this pattern we have identified 15 loci which fall into three groups. Mutations at six 'segment polarity' loci cause pattern duplications within each segment, those at six 'pair-rule' loci delete homologous parts in every other segment, and those at three 'gap' loci remove a continuous stretch of adjacent segments.
