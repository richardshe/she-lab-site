Inflammatory caspases (caspase-1, caspase-4, caspase-5 and caspase-11) are essential effectors of antimicrobial host defence and lethal endotoxemia, where they drive pyroptosis and the maturation of interleukin (IL)-1 family cytokines. Cytoplasmic lipopolysaccharide (LPS) directly engages caspase-11 (or its human orthologues caspase-4 and caspase-5) to trigger pyroptosis and non-canonical NLRP3-dependent activation of caspase-1, yet how these caspases execute cell death has remained unknown. Here we use an unbiased ethyl N-nitrosourea mutagenesis screen and gene-targeted mice to identify gasdermin D (Gsdmd) as an indispensable mediator of non-canonical inflammasome signalling. Caspase-11 and caspase-4 directly cleave Gsdmd at a conserved Asp-Gly motif, liberating an N-terminal p30 fragment that is sufficient to induce pyroptosis and that licenses NLRP3-dependent caspase-1 activation through a cell-intrinsic route. Gsdmd is also a physiological substrate of caspase-1 and is required for the lethality of LPS-induced septic shock in vivo, identifying Gsdmd as a central effector of inflammatory caspase activity.
