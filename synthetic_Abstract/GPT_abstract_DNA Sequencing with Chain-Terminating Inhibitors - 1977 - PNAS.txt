A method is described for determining DNA sequences by use of DNA polymerase and chain-terminating inhibitors. 2',3'-Dideoxy and arabinosyl triphosphates, when incorporated in place of the corresponding normal nucleotides, stop further extension of a primer on a template. Separate reactions for the four bases give sets of labelled fragments which, after electrophoresis on denaturing acrylamide gels, can be read as sequence. Examples from phiX174 DNA show accurate readings and reveal corrections to the provisional sequence.
