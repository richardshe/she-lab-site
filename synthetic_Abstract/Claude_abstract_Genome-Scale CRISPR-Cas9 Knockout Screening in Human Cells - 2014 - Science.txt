The bacterial CRISPR-Cas9 system has emerged as a versatile tool for introducing targeted loss-of-function mutations in mammalian cells, but its potential for pooled, genome-scale functional screening has yet to be realized. Here we report a lentiviral single-guide RNA (sgRNA) library targeting 5' constitutive exons of 18,080 genes in the human genome, with 3-4 sgRNAs per gene, that enables both negative and positive selection screening in human cells. We used this genome-scale CRISPR-Cas9 knockout (GeCKO) library to identify genes essential for cell viability in a melanoma cell line and a pluripotent stem cell line, and to screen for genes whose loss confers resistance to the BRAF kinase inhibitor vemurafenib. Our highest-ranking candidates include previously validated genes NF1 and MED12, as well as novel hits NF2, CUL3, TADA1, and TADA2B. GeCKO offers a robust and reliable alternative to RNAi for systematic perturbation of gene function in mammalian cells.
