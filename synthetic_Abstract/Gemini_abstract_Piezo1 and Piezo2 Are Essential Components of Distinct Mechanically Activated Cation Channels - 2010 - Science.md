# Abstract

Mechanotransduction is fundamental to diverse physiological processes, including touch, pain, and hearing. Despite the widespread characterization of mechanically activated (MA) cationic currents in mammalian cells, the molecular identities of the underlying ion channels have remained elusive. Here, we identify Piezo1 (Fam38A) as an essential component of MA currents in a mouse neuroblastoma cell line. Piezo1 and its paralog Piezo2 (Fam38B) encode large, evolutionarily conserved transmembrane proteins lacking homology to known ion channels. Heterologous overexpression of Piezo1 or Piezo2 generates massive MA cationic currents with distinct kinetic properties. Furthermore, knockdown of Piezo2 in dorsal root ganglion sensory neurons specifically eliminates a subset of rapidly adapting MA currents. We conclude that Piezo1 and Piezo2 are necessary and sufficient to form distinct MA cation channels, defining a novel class of mechanotransduction proteins in mammals.
