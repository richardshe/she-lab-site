Eukaryotic gene regulation is encoded in chromatin accessibility, nucleosome organization and transcription factor occupancy, yet existing genome-wide assays require large cell numbers and separate, time-consuming protocols. Here we describe assay for transposase-accessible chromatin using sequencing (ATAC-seq), a rapid method in which adaptor-loaded Tn5 transposase preferentially inserts into accessible native chromatin. ATAC-seq profiles open chromatin from as few as hundreds of nuclei, recapitulates DNase-seq maps, and yields paired-end fragment distributions that disclose nucleosome-free and nucleosome-bound regulatory regions. Local depletion of insertions over transcription factor motifs further provides footprints that infer factor occupancy genome wide. Applied to primary human blood cells, ATAC-seq generated personalized regulatory information on clinical timescales, enabling integrated analysis of chromatin state, nucleosome positioning and regulatory networks from limited samples.
