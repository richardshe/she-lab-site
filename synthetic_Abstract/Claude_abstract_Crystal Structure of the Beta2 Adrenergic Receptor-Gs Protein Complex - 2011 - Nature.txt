G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) constitute the largest family of cell-surface signalling proteins, transducing extracellular stimuli into intracellular responses through heterotrimeric G proteins. Despite decades of biochemical and pharmacological characterization of the β2 adrenergic receptor (β2AR) and its stimulatory G protein Gs, the structural basis for receptor-mediated G protein activation has remained elusive. Here we present the crystal structure of the agonist-occupied β2AR in complex with the nucleotide-free Gs heterotrimer at 3.2 Å resolution. To obtain diffraction-quality crystals, the unstructured amino terminus of the β2AR was replaced with T4 lysozyme, the complex was stabilized with the high-affinity agonist BI-167107, and a camelid nanobody (Nb35) was used to lock the interface between the Gα and Gβ subunits. The structure reveals a 14 Å outward movement of transmembrane helix 6 and substantial rearrangements at the cytoplasmic surface of the receptor, which together create a cavity that accommodates the carboxy-terminal α5-helix of Gαs. Most strikingly, the α-helical domain of Gαs is rotated approximately 127° relative to its Ras-like domain, exposing the empty nucleotide-binding pocket. These observations provide a structural framework for understanding transmembrane signalling and nucleotide exchange in GPCR-G protein complexes.
