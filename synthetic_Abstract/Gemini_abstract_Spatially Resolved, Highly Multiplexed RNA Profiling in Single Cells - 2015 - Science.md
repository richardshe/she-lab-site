Characterizing the spatial organization and abundance of RNAs in single cells is critical for understanding cellular heterogeneity and gene regulation. However, standard single-molecule fluorescence in situ hybridization (smFISH) is fundamentally limited in its multiplexing capacity. Here, we introduce multiplexed error-robust fluorescence in situ hybridization (MERFISH), a highly scalable imaging method that significantly expands the number of RNA species quantifiable in single cells. MERFISH utilizes combinatorial labeling and sequential imaging, protected by error-detecting and error-correcting code schemes. We demonstrate this approach by simultaneously profiling 140 RNA species with an error-correcting code, and 1,001 RNAs with an error-detecting code in human fibroblasts. This massive single-cell dataset enabled us to analyze stochastic gene expression variations, discover co-regulated gene networks, and systematically map the distinct subcellular spatial distributions of numerous transcripts. MERFISH provides a robust platform for highly multiplexed, spatially resolved in situ transcriptomics.
